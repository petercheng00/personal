Indoor Texture Mapping


I spent 3 years at the Video and Image Processing Lab, working on automatically generating 3d models of indoor environments. This was accomplished by walking around a multitude of indoor locations while wearing a backpack-mounted sensor suite for data acquisition. My focus was on efficient texture mapping given noisy camera poses and geometry. A few samples of the types of models I generated are on this page.

My work was presented at IS&T/SPIE 2013 and 2014, and was the basis of my Master's thesis.

Myopia/Sunlight Research


I also spent 1 year at the Wildoset Vision Sciences Lab, where I performed analysis on eye shape (related to myopia) and sunlight, attempting to find a correlation between the two. This was accomplished via a head-mounted eye-measuring device and light sensor.
